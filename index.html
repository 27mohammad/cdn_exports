Rices

There are many types of rice, ranging from long-grain to short-grain and from fragrant to sticky.

Aromatic Basmati Rice: Fragrant and Flavorful

Basmati rice is a long-grain aromatic rice that is commonly grown in the Indian subcontinent. It has a distinct nutty flavor and floral aroma, and is often used in dishes such as biryanis, pilafs, and curries.

Nutritious Black Rice: Dark and Delicious

lack rice, also known as forbidden rice, is a type of rice with a dark purplish-black color. It is highly nutritious and rich in antioxidants, fiber, and minerals. Black rice has a slightly sweet, nutty flavor and a slightly chewy texture

Versatile Broken Rice: Budget-Friendly and Easy to Cook

Broken rice is a type of rice that has been broken into smaller pieces during the milling process. It is typically less expensive than whole grain rice and cooks faster, making it a popular choice for a variety of dishes.

Healthy Brown Rice: Nutritious and Delicious

Brown rice is a whole grain rice that has not had its bran and germ removed, making it a more nutritious alternative to white rice. It is a good source of fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals. Brown rice has a nutty flavor and a slightly chewy texture, and is a versatile ingredient.

Flavorful Chinnor Rice: Perfect for Biryani and Pulao

Chinnaur rice is a high-quality, aromatic rice that is predominantly grown in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It has long, slender grains with a delicate texture and a distinct flavor that sets it apart from other varieties of rice.

Mushrooms

Mushrooms are a type of fungi that offer a rich earthy flavor, and are packed with important nutrients and health benefits.

Flammulina filiformis is a species of agaric (gilled mushroom) in the family Physalacriaceae. It is well known for its role in Japanese cuisine, where it is called enokitake.

Inocybe hystrix is an agaric fungus in the family Inocybaceae. It forms mycorrhiza with surrounding deciduous trees. Fruit bodies are usually found growing alone or in small groups on leaf litter during autumn months.

Hypholoma is a genus of fungi which are quite well known due to the commonness of sulphur tuft (Hypholoma fasciculare) on stumps in temperate woodlands.

Stropharia aeruginosa is commonly known as the verdigris agaric, is a medium-sized green, slimy woodland mushroom, found on lawns, mulch and woodland from spring to autumn.

Stropharia rugosoannulata is commonly known as the wine cap stropharia, "garden giant", burgundy mushroom or king stropharia, is an agaric of the family Strophariaceae found in Europe and North America, and introduced to Australia and New Zealand.

Ganoderma is a genus of polypore fungi in the family Ganodermataceae that includes about 80 species, many from tropical regions. Because of their genetic diversity, use in traditional Asian medicines, and potential in bioremediation, they are an important genus economically.
